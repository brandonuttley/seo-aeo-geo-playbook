# SEO / AEO / GEO Playbook

Most content strategy guides treat SEO, AEO, and GEO as variations of the same problem. They aren't.

Each discipline targets a different system, rewards different writing behaviors, and fails in different ways when ignored. Lumping them together produces content that's optimized for nothing in particular.

This playbook separates the three — then shows how to combine them deliberately.

---

## Who this is for

- **Marketing generalists** who need to understand the strategic differences before delegating execution
- **Content writers** who want to know why the same article ranks well on Google but never gets cited by AI systems
- **SEO specialists** expanding their practice into answer engines and generative AI

If you already know the basics of SEO, you're ready for this. If AEO and GEO are newer territory, start with the framework doc before moving to the modules.

---

## The core argument

Search has fractured into three distinct surfaces:

- **Search engines** (Google, Bing) rank pages based on authority, relevance, and technical signals
- **Answer engines** (featured snippets, voice search, People Also Ask) extract direct answers from structured content
- **Generative engines** (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews) synthesize and cite sources based on credibility, structure, and entity clarity

Writing for one doesn't guarantee performance in the others. A page can dominate traditional rankings while remaining invisible to AI systems — because AI systems don't reward the same signals.

The solution isn't to write three versions of everything. It's to understand what each surface needs, then layer those needs intentionally.

## How to use the modules

The modules are designed to be combined, not applied all at once.

A typical workflow:

1. Run **Tier 1** on everything — it covers the signals all three surfaces share
2. Add one or two **Tier 2** modules based on your content format
3. Add a **Tier 3** module if you're targeting a specific platform

This keeps optimization focused. It also makes it easier to diagnose problems — if something isn't performing, you know which layer to look at.
