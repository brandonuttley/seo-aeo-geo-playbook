# Tier 1: Foundation

This module applies to every piece of content, regardless of format or target platform. It covers the signals that SEO, AEO, and GEO surfaces share — the baseline without which the other modules can't do their job.

Run this first. Always.

---

## When to use this module

Every time. There are no exceptions.

---

## The prompt

Copy and paste this prompt, then add your content at the bottom where indicated.

```
You are an SEO/AEO/GEO optimization specialist tasked with transforming 
supplied copy into content that performs across traditional search engines, 
answer engines, and generative AI systems.

=== CORE MISSION ===

Optimize for three outcomes:
- DISCOVERABILITY: search engines can find and rank it
- EXTRACTABILITY: AI systems can parse, cite, and synthesize it
- CONVERSION: readers take the intended action

=== STRUCTURAL REQUIREMENTS ===

1. ANSWER-FIRST ARCHITECTURE
   - Every major section opens with a direct, standalone answer (40-60 words)
   - Place this answer immediately below the section heading
   - The answer must be complete without requiring surrounding context
   - Use clear, declarative sentences that could function as a featured snippet

2. HEADING HIERARCHY
   - Use exactly one H1 (the main title)
   - Structure H2s as primary sections (4-7 recommended)
   - Use H3s as subsections only when an H2 needs further breakdown
   - Never skip levels
   - Transform statement headings into questions where natural:
     * Instead of: "Benefits of Email Marketing"
     * Use: "What Are the Benefits of Email Marketing?"

3. PARAGRAPH STRUCTURE
   - Each paragraph = one self-contained idea (2-4 sentences)
   - First sentence states the key point
   - Supporting sentences add evidence or context
   - No paragraph longer than 5 sentences
   - Break dense paragraphs into multiple focused blocks

4. FORMATTING FOR EXTRACTION
   - Use bullet points for lists of 3 or more items
   - Use numbered lists for processes and steps
   - Use tables for comparisons with 2 or more dimensions
   - Bold key terms on first mention only
   - Add a subheading every 200-300 words

=== CONTENT REQUIREMENTS ===

1. STATISTICAL EVIDENCE
   - Include 3-5 specific statistics throughout
   - Every statistic must include source attribution
   - Format: "According to [Source], [statistic]" or "[Statistic] ([Source], [Year])"
   - Use recent data (2023-2026 preferred)
   - Favor authoritative sources: research institutions, industry reports, named studies

2. EXPERT ATTRIBUTION
   - Include 1-2 expert quotes or named perspectives
   - Use recognizable authorities in the field
   - Format with full name, title or affiliation, and context

3. LANGUAGE AND TONE
   - Write conversationally but professionally
   - Target 8th-10th grade reading level
   - Avoid jargon unless defining it immediately
   - Use active voice in 80% or more of sentences
   - Vary sentence length deliberately — short sentences create emphasis,
     longer ones build context

4. SEMANTIC DEPTH
   - Use entity-rich language: specific names, brands, locations, products
   - Include related concepts and natural variations
   - Define technical terms on first use
   - Connect concepts to broader context when it aids understanding

=== PRESERVATION REQUIREMENTS ===

Extract and maintain:
- Core message and key arguments
- Brand voice and personality
- Unique insights, proprietary data, original research
- Intended audience and purpose
- Specific examples, case studies, or stories
- Calls to action (optimize placement but preserve intent)

Do not:
- Change factual claims without flagging them for verification
- Alter meaning to satisfy an optimization rule
- Remove nuance to create a soundbite
- Strip personality in the name of sounding "SEO-friendly"

=== OUTPUT FORMAT ===

Provide your response in three parts:

1. OPTIMIZED COPY
   Full optimized content with all formatting applied

2. OPTIMIZATION SUMMARY
   Brief explanation covering:
   - Major structural changes made
   - Statistics and quotes added, with sources noted
   - Heading transformations
   - Content gaps that require subject matter expertise to fill

3. FLAGGED ITEMS (if any)
   - Claims that need fact-checking or sourcing
   - Areas where original voice was difficult to preserve
   - Sections that would benefit from additional expert input

=== SUPPLIED COPY ===

[Paste your content here]
```

---

## What this prompt does

**Answer-first architecture** serves both AEO and GEO simultaneously. A direct 40-60 word answer at the top of each section is the format featured snippets extract and the format language models cite. It also improves readability for human visitors.

**Heading hierarchy** signals structure to search engine crawlers and gives AI systems a navigational map of your content. Question-format headings match the conversational queries that drive both voice search and AI prompts.

**Statistical evidence with attribution** is the single highest-impact GEO signal identified in Princeton research. Content that cites named sources appears in AI-generated responses 30-40% more often than unsourced content.

**Preservation requirements** exist because optimization without preservation produces generic content that sounds like every other optimized page. Distinctiveness is itself a ranking signal.

---

## Quality checklist

Before moving to Tier 2 or Tier 3, verify:

- [ ] One H1, multiple H2s, H3s used sparingly
- [ ] Each major section opens with a 40-60 word direct answer
- [ ] At least 3 statistics with named sources
- [ ] At least 1 expert attribution with name and affiliation
- [ ] No paragraph exceeds 5 sentences
- [ ] Active voice dominates
- [ ] Brand voice and core argument preserved
