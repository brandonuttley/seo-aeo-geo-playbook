# Combined Examples

The modules in this playbook are designed to be assembled, not used in isolation. This file shows four common scenarios with the modules combined and ready to use.

Each example includes the assembled prompt and a brief explanation of why those modules were chosen.

---

## How to use these examples

1. Find the scenario closest to your content type and goal
2. Copy the full assembled prompt
3. Replace the placeholder text in `[brackets]` with your specifics
4. Paste your content at the bottom where indicated
5. Run it in your AI tool of choice

## Example 1: Blog post targeting featured snippets

**Scenario:** A 1,200-word blog post answering one primary question. Goal is to rank on Google and appear as a featured snippet.

**Modules used:** Tier 1 Foundation + Tier 2 Module A (Featured Snippet)

```
You are an SEO/AEO/GEO optimization specialist tasked with transforming 
supplied copy into content that performs across traditional search engines, 
answer engines, and generative AI systems.

=== CORE MISSION ===

Optimize for three outcomes:
- DISCOVERABILITY: search engines can find and rank it
- EXTRACTABILITY: AI systems can parse, cite, and synthesize it
- CONVERSION: readers take the intended action

=== STRUCTURAL REQUIREMENTS ===

1. ANSWER-FIRST ARCHITECTURE
   - Every major section opens with a direct, standalone answer (40-60 words)
   - Place this answer immediately below the section heading
   - The answer must be complete without requiring surrounding context

2. HEADING HIERARCHY
   - Use exactly one H1
   - Structure H2s as primary sections (4-7 recommended)
   - Use H3s as subsections only when needed
   - Transform statement headings into questions where natural

3. PARAGRAPH STRUCTURE
   - Each paragraph = one self-contained idea (2-4 sentences)
   - No paragraph longer than 5 sentences

4. FORMATTING FOR EXTRACTION
   - Bullet points for lists of 3 or more items
   - Numbered lists for processes and steps
   - Tables for comparisons with 2 or more dimensions
   - Subheading every 200-300 words

=== CONTENT REQUIREMENTS ===

- Include 3-5 statistics with named source attribution
- Include 1-2 expert quotes with full name and affiliation
- Active voice in 80%+ of sentences
- 8th-10th grade reading level
- Preserve original brand voice and core argument

=== FEATURED SNIPPET OPTIMIZATION ===

Identify the single primary question this content answers.

Place a 42-50 word paragraph answer directly below the H2 that poses 
that question. This answer must be complete without surrounding context.

For process content: numbered lists, 3-8 items, action verb to start each.
For comparison content: table with 2-4 columns, max 5 rows.

Add at least one "What is [topic]?" section and one "How to [action]?" 
section if not already present.

=== OUTPUT FORMAT ===

1. OPTIMIZED COPY
2. OPTIMIZATION SUMMARY (major changes, sources added, heading changes)
3. FLAGGED ITEMS (claims needing verification, voice preservation issues)

=== SUPPLIED COPY ===

```

## Example 2: FAQ page targeting People Also Ask

**Scenario:** A standalone FAQ page covering multiple related questions. Goal is to appear in Google's People Also Ask clusters and voice search results.

**Modules used:** Tier 1 Foundation + Tier 2 Module B (FAQ / PAA)

```
You are an SEO/AEO/GEO optimization specialist tasked with transforming 
supplied copy into content that performs across traditional search engines, 
answer engines, and generative AI systems.

=== CORE MISSION ===

Optimize for three outcomes:
- DISCOVERABILITY: search engines can find and rank it
- EXTRACTABILITY: AI systems can parse, cite, and synthesize it
- CONVERSION: readers take the intended action

=== STRUCTURAL REQUIREMENTS ===

1. ANSWER-FIRST ARCHITECTURE
   - Every section opens with a direct, standalone answer (40-60 words)

2. HEADING HIERARCHY
   - One H1, multiple H2s, H3s used sparingly
   - Question-format headings throughout

3. PARAGRAPH STRUCTURE
   - Each paragraph = one self-contained idea (2-4 sentences)

4. FORMATTING FOR EXTRACTION
   - Bullet points, numbered lists, and tables where appropriate
   - Subheading every 200-300 words

=== CONTENT REQUIREMENTS ===

- 3-5 statistics with named source attribution
- 1-2 expert quotes with full name and affiliation
- Active voice, 8th-10th grade reading level
- Preserve original brand voice

=== FAQ / PEOPLE ALSO ASK OPTIMIZATION ===

Generate 5-8 questions a reader would ask about this topic.

Format each as an H3 heading phrased as a natural question.
Place a 40-60 word direct answer immediately below each heading.

Requirements:
- Cluster questions by subtopic
- Include questions from awareness, consideration, and decision stages
- Answers must be self-contained — no "As mentioned above..."
- Each answer introduces at least one entity (name, brand, statistic)

## Example 3: Resource article targeting AI citation

**Scenario:** A long-form resource article on a topic where you want to be cited by ChatGPT and Perplexity when users ask related questions.

**Modules used:** Tier 1 Foundation + Tier 2 Module B (FAQ / PAA) + Tier 3 Module A (ChatGPT / Perplexity)

```
You are an SEO/AEO/GEO optimization specialist tasked with transforming 
supplied copy into content that performs across traditional search engines, 
answer engines, and generative AI systems.

=== CORE MISSION ===

Optimize for three outcomes:
- DISCOVERABILITY: search engines can find and rank it
- EXTRACTABILITY: AI systems can parse, cite, and synthesize it
- CONVERSION: readers take the intended action

=== STRUCTURAL REQUIREMENTS ===

1. ANSWER-FIRST ARCHITECTURE
   - Every section opens with a direct, standalone answer (40-60 words)

2. HEADING HIERARCHY
   - One H1, multiple H2s, H3s as needed
   - Question-format headings throughout

3. PARAGRAPH STRUCTURE
   - Each paragraph = one self-contained, citable idea (2-4 sentences)

4. FORMATTING FOR EXTRACTION
   - Bullet points, numbered lists, tables where appropriate
   - Subheading every 200-300 words

=== CONTENT REQUIREMENTS ===

- 3-5 statistics with named source attribution
- 1-2 expert quotes with full name and affiliation
- Active voice, 8th-10th grade reading level
- Preserve original brand voice

=== FAQ / PEOPLE ALSO ASK OPTIMIZATION ===

Generate 5-8 questions a reader would ask about this topic.
Format each as an H3 question heading with a 40-60 word answer below.
Cluster by subtopic. Include awareness, consideration, and decision questions.
Add a dedicated FAQ section at the end.

=== GENERATIVE ENGINE OPTIMIZATION (GEO) — ChatGPT / Perplexity ===

Source citation requirements:
- Every major claim backed by a named source
- Format: "According to [Organization], [claim]."
- Include publication year where available

Expert attribution:
- 2-3 quotes from recognized authorities
- Full name, title, organization for each

Statistical density:
- At least 5 specific statistics with units and dates
- At least one comparison statistic ("X vs. Y")

Entity requirements:
- Use proper nouns throughout — name entities specifically
- Define acronyms on first use

Freshness signals:
- At least one date reference ("as of 2026")
- Avoid vague evergreen language

Structure for citability:
- Each paragraph contains one extractable, citable claim
- Key facts in their own sentences, not buried in complex clauses

=== OUTPUT FORMAT ===

1. OPTIMIZED COPY
2. OPTIMIZATION SUMMARY
3. FLAGGED ITEMS

=== SUPPLIED COPY ===

## Example 4: Product or service page targeting Google AI Overviews

**Scenario:** A product or service page for something already ranking in Google's top 10. Goal is to also appear in Google AI Overviews for high-intent queries.

**Modules used:** Tier 1 Foundation + Tier 2 Module D (Product / Service) + Tier 3 Module B (Google AI Overviews)

```
You are an SEO/AEO/GEO optimization specialist tasked with transforming 
supplied copy into content that performs across traditional search engines, 
answer engines, and generative AI systems.

=== CORE MISSION ===

Optimize for three outcomes:
- DISCOVERABILITY: search engines can find and rank it
- EXTRACTABILITY: AI systems can parse, cite, and synthesize it
- CONVERSION: readers take the intended action

=== STRUCTURAL REQUIREMENTS ===

1. ANSWER-FIRST ARCHITECTURE
   - Every section opens with a direct, standalone answer (40-60 words)
   - Lead with the primary benefit, not the feature

2. HEADING HIERARCHY
   - One H1, multiple H2s, H3s as needed
   - Include "What is [product/service]?" and "How does it work?" sections

3. PARAGRAPH STRUCTURE
   - Each paragraph = one self-contained idea (2-4 sentences)
   - Key sentences under 20 words for AI extraction

4. FORMATTING FOR EXTRACTION
   - Comparison table: your offering vs. 1-2 alternatives
   - Pros/cons section
   - Numbered steps for any process
   - Subheading every 200-300 words

=== CONTENT REQUIREMENTS ===

- 3-5 statistics with named source attribution
- Performance claims include source or basis ("based on customer data")
- Active voice, 8th-10th grade reading level
- Single, clear call to action

=== PRODUCT / SERVICE PAGE OPTIMIZATION ===

- Lead with primary benefit, not feature
- Comparison table: your offering vs. 1-2 alternatives
- Pros/cons section — honest cons improve credibility
- FAQ section addressing the 5 most common objections
- State intended audience explicitly
- Single call to action — no competing next steps
- Flag pricing, reviews, and FAQs for schema markup

=== AI OVERVIEW OPTIMIZATION — Google ===

- Place the most important answer in the first 100 words
- Include E-E-A-T signals: author credentials, firsthand experience,
  authoritative external citations, last-updated date
- Use clear "What is X?" and "How does X work?" sections
- Step-by-step processes as numbered lists
- Tables for any data with 2+ comparable dimensions
- Keep sentences in key sections under 20 words
- Flag content for schema markup: Article, FAQPage, HowTo, Product

=== OUTPUT FORMAT ===

1. OPTIMIZED COPY
2. OPTIMIZATION SUMMARY
3. FLAGGED ITEMS

=== SUPPLIED COPY ===

[Paste your product or service page content here]
```
